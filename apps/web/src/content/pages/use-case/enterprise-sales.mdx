## Page three of the vendor questionnaire

You're in the final stages of an enterprise deal. Their security team sends a vendor questionnaire. Somewhere on page three: **"Do you have a public status page? Provide the URL."**

If your answer is "no" or a blank field, you just added friction to a deal that was nearly closed.

## Enterprise buyers are buying transparency

Enterprise customers don't just buy your product — they buy **your reliability story**. They need to prove to their own stakeholders that every vendor they depend on is transparent about uptime, incidents, and maintenance.

A status page tells them three things:
- **You monitor your own systems** — reliability isn't an afterthought
- **You communicate proactively** — outages aren't swept under the rug
- **You have a process** — incidents are detected, communicated, and resolved systematically

Without one, they're taking your word for it. With one, they have proof.

## What closes the deal

### Verified uptime history

Your status page shows **real uptime data** powered by synthetic monitoring from **28 global regions**. Not a self-reported "100% uptime" claim — actual, verifiable numbers. Procurement teams trust data, not promises.

### Incident track record

Every **status report** is timestamped and permanent. Buyers can see how you've handled past incidents — how fast you detected them, how you communicated, and how you resolved them. Your incident history becomes your strongest reference.

### Branded custom domain

Host on `status.yourcompany.com`. A custom domain signals your status page is a first-class part of your product, not a checkbox you filled in last week.

### Dedicated client pages

Create **password-protected status pages** for enterprise clients. Show only the components relevant to their account. Your largest customers get dedicated visibility — and feel like a priority.

### Proactive subscriber notifications

Enterprise clients **subscribe via email** and get updates the moment something changes. No checking, no guessing, no "why didn't you tell us?" conversations.
