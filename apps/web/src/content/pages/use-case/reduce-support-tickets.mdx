## "Is it down?" — the ticket you get 200 times during an outage

When your service goes down, users don't wait. They open tickets, send emails, ping your chat support, and post on social media — all asking the same question: **"Is it down?"**

Your support team spends the entire incident answering that question instead of helping resolve the issue. Meanwhile, users who don't contact support assume the worst.

## Give every user one place to check

A public status page answers "is it down?" before anyone asks. Users check your status page, see the incident is acknowledged, and wait for updates — instead of flooding your inbox.

Fewer tickets during outages. Less pressure on your support team. Users who trust you're handling it.

## What makes ticket deflection work

### Detect before users report

Connect **synthetic monitors** to your status page components. When a monitor catches an issue, you know before your users do. Monitor from **28 global regions** to catch regional problems early — before a wave of tickets tells you something is wrong.

### Timestamped status reports

Publish a **status report** the moment you detect an issue. Add updates as you investigate, identify the cause, and resolve it. Every update is timestamped — users see progress without asking.

### Proactive subscriber notifications

Let users **subscribe via email, RSS, or JSON feeds** so they hear from you first. Proactive communication is the difference between "they're on it" and "do they even know?"

### Component-level granularity

Break your status page into **components** — API, dashboard, billing, webhooks. When only one service is affected, users of other services see everything else is operational. No unnecessary tickets from users whose workflows aren't impacted.

### Scheduled maintenance announcements

Announce maintenance windows in advance. Subscribers are notified before the work begins — so the tickets that would have come in during the window never get created.
